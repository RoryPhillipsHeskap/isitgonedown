Why is Facebook not working?

1. A Meta outage

Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp and Messenger all share Meta infrastructure. A major Meta outage takes all four down at once (most famously in October 2021, for six hours).

2. News Feed loading forever

A stuck News Feed usually means Meta's feed service is degraded. Reads are slow or failing even though the core site loads. Wait 10 minutes or try the mobile app.

3. Can't post or comment

Write operations (posts, comments, reactions) can fail while reads work. This is a common partial outage pattern. Try later or via mobile.

4. Account locked or limited

If Facebook loads but shows "Action Blocked" messages or locks you out, the issue is account-specific, not a Facebook outage. Check your notifications and account support tools.

5. Network block

Many employers and schools block Facebook. If Facebook fails on one network but works on another, your network is filtering it.

6. Browser or app cache

A corrupted cache can cause errors that mimic an outage. Clearing cookies for facebook.com or clearing the Facebook app cache usually fixes it.

Is Facebook down for everyone or just me?

A good signal: if Messenger also fails, it's almost certainly a Meta-wide issue. If Messenger works but Facebook doesn't, the problem is either a partial outage or on your side.

What to do if Facebook is only down for you

  • Try mobile web. m.facebook.com on a phone browser bypasses the main site's cached state.
  • Clear cookies. For facebook.com specifically — a bad session cookie breaks many things silently.
  • Log out and back in. Refreshes your session.
  • Check Messenger. If Messenger works, your Facebook account may be temporarily restricted.
  • Switch networks. Mobile data vs Wi-Fi — isolates network blocks.
  • Update the app. Old versions can silently break when Meta updates the backend.
