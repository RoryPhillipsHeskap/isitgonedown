About IsItGoneDown.com

The free, community-powered website status checker — built to answer one simple question: is it down for everyone, or just you?

IsItGoneDown.com is operated by Heskap IT Support, based in Ireland. We built this tool because we were tired of wondering whether a website outage was on our end or happening globally — and wanted a fast, honest answer with no signup required.

What we do

IsItGoneDown.com checks whether any website is genuinely down for everyone around the world, or whether the problem is specific to your connection, ISP, or device. Every check is instant and completely free.

We run two tests simultaneously:

  • Global server check — we send a request through a third-party proxy server to test whether the site is reachable from outside your network.
  • Local browser check — your browser attempts to reach the site directly, revealing whether the issue is local to you.

Together, these two checks tell you one of three things: the site is down for everyone, it's down just for you, or it's partially reachable (which can indicate regional outages or CDN issues).

Why we built it

When a website goes down, the first question is always: "Is this just me?" Most people turn to social media or search engines, which are slow and unreliable sources of outage information. We wanted a dedicated tool that gives you a definitive answer in seconds, without ads, paywalls, or signups.

IsItGoneDown.com launched in 2026 and is actively maintained and improved. We monitor feedback from our community and push updates regularly.

How community voting works

After running a check, you can vote whether a site is up or down for you. These votes are aggregated in real time and displayed alongside the technical check result. When many users report a site as down, it's a strong signal of a genuine outage — even if the server responds to automated tests (which some sites do even during degraded service).

Community reports are especially useful for detecting partial outages, login issues, performance problems, and app-layer failures that a basic connectivity check might miss.

Who is this for?

IsItGoneDown.com is useful for anyone who relies on websites and online services in their daily life or work:

  • Everyday users — quickly check whether a streaming service, social platform, or shopping site is having problems before troubleshooting your own connection.
  • Remote workers — verify whether a work tool (Slack, Zoom, Microsoft 365) is down globally before contacting IT support.
  • Developers and sysadmins — use bulk checking to monitor a list of services and get email alerts when something goes offline.
  • Small businesses — confirm whether a supplier's site or payment gateway is experiencing downtime.
