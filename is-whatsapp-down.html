Why is WhatsApp not working?

1. A Meta outage

WhatsApp shares core infrastructure with Facebook, Instagram and Messenger. When Meta has an incident, WhatsApp is usually affected alongside the others. A sudden surge in reports across all four apps is a strong signal Meta itself is down.

2. Your phone lost connectivity

WhatsApp needs a constant internet connection on the phone itself — not just on WhatsApp Web. If your phone briefly drops Wi-Fi or mobile data, messages stop sending even if Web is open on your laptop.

3. WhatsApp Web session expired

WhatsApp Web disconnects whenever your phone loses signal for too long. Re-scan the QR code from WhatsApp → Linked Devices to restore the session.

4. Country-level block

WhatsApp is blocked in a handful of countries and throttled on some corporate, school and public networks. If WhatsApp is only unreachable on one network, this is almost always why.

5. Number banned or account restricted

If WhatsApp immediately boots you out with a "Your phone number is banned" message, the problem is account-specific. Contact WhatsApp support directly.

6. Outdated app version

WhatsApp occasionally retires old app versions. If the app suddenly won't open at all and everyone else's WhatsApp works fine, update from the app store.

Is WhatsApp down for everyone or just me?

If WhatsApp is down globally, your messages will still queue and deliver automatically when service returns. If WhatsApp is only down for you, it's usually your phone's connection or the WhatsApp Web session.

IsItGoneDown tests WhatsApp from both a server outside your network and your browser, so you instantly know which side is the problem.

What to do if WhatsApp is only down for you

  • Toggle Airplane Mode. Turn it on for 10 seconds, then off. Forces your phone to re-establish its connection to WhatsApp's servers.
  • Close and reopen the app. Swipe WhatsApp out of recent apps and relaunch.
  • Check WhatsApp Web. If Web works but mobile doesn't, the issue is on your phone. If Web also fails, WhatsApp is likely down globally.
  • Re-link WhatsApp Web. Go to WhatsApp → Linked Devices and scan the QR code again.
  • Switch from Wi-Fi to mobile data. Isolates whether it's the network at fault.
  • Update the app. Older versions are silently retired.

Frequently asked questions

Is WhatsApp down right now?

Use the button above to run a live check. You'll see global status instantly plus community reports from other WhatsApp users.

Why is WhatsApp not sending messages?

The cause is usually either a Meta-side outage, a drop on your phone's network, or the app needing an update. Queued messages deliver automatically when WhatsApp comes back.

Is WhatsApp Web down?

Web needs both WhatsApp's servers and your phone online. If Web says 'Phone not connected', your phone lost its link.

Will my messages send when WhatsApp comes back?

Yes — outgoing messages are queued locally and delivered automatically once service returns. You'll see ticks update.

Does a VPN help when WhatsApp is blocked?

Yes — for country- or network-level blocks, a VPN routes your traffic through a different region and restores access. A VPN won't help if WhatsApp itself is down.
